How to Do the Mordern Farmhouse

This look takes the best of “rustic,” a touch of industrial, and the sleek elegance of “modern.”

written by Rebekah Carey

In many parts of America, a major trend in domestic architecture is the modern farmhouse style. Overall, this aesthetic is modeled on traditional farmhouses, and defined by a spacious, functional layout designed to accommodate large groups of people and livable, practical interiors. It’s sophisticated, without feeling pretentious or unapproachable, and it’s inviting enough that you feel like you can kick back and relax.

Exterior

To get the modern farmhouse look on your exterior, crisp paint colors are key. Black, white, natural wood, or a combination of the three are typically seen. Here the “country” elements of board and batten are off-set with a high gloss black painted door, modern house numbers and an industrial-inspired light fixture.

Living Space

In this home you can again see the main colors are a combination of black (modern), and white and wood (earthy elements). Texture is added in with the leather of the couch and the softness of the rug, along with the cool elements of the metal.

Country Bathroom

Aged brick, especially in a chevron pattern, adds warmth in the tone and a rustic vibe from the texture. We like that the brick is the only warmer color in the space, too.

Polished

The best part about the Modern Farmhouse look is that there isn’t a particular percentage that you have to do each part in. If you want the rustic elements to be featured less than the industrial, that’s fine! Here modern and industrial are showcased with little pops of wood and other rustic elements sprinkled about.

Polished Garage

Painted brick, crisp black details in the trim and light fixture, the gorgeous patterned garage doors feel rustic, but modern, with their clean lines and neat windows.

Refined Farmhouse

Modern farmhouse style also does not have to be rustic! How to create an elegant and soft look while still featuring the main tenants of the style? White wood paneling, warm wood furniture and flooring, clean lined pieces are all staples, but adding soft fabrics, warm colors, and textures into the space creates a different take.

Storage

Modern farmhouse style can also be subtle! We love the small industrial lights that run the width of these shelves. The crisp white paint is warmed by the beautiful wooden worn floors and elevated by the modern lights.

Kitchen Dreams

We love a good farm table kitchen island! The high ceilings painted white, the beautifully aged farm table, and the industrial inspired lights are all the stuff of dreams!

Mix and Match

We love mixing different eras into our living spaces. Here the crips white walls, rustic farm table and black industrial light all check the modern farmhouse boxes, but they’re made more relaxed with the addition of 60s chairs, pieces in different scales, and a colorful rug over the polished concrete floors.

Sliding In Style

Sliding “barn doors” are a definite staple in the modern farmhouse look. They’re able to remain modern feeling by the high-gloss black paint and the industrial hardware.

Modern Country

These amazingly tall ceilings could have been left their natural wood color, but painting them all white showcases just how high they are. We love the drama that’s added from how long the light fixtures get to be. Bleached wood floors feel the slightest bit rustic, and completely welcoming.
